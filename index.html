BloomTimer

A blooming Pomodoro timer that rewards your focus with light and movement.

BloomTimer is a physical focus timer that blooms as you work, giving you a small reward for staying with your task instead of reaching for your phone.

TECHIN 511 · Global Innovation Exchange · Final Project

Description · Purpose · Intended users · Design & functionality

Product Overview

Purpose. Our project addresses the problem that people often find it hard to clearly understand and keep track of what is happening in their daily activities. By turning abstract information into a clear, visual and interactive form, the product helps users notice patterns and make better decisions. The built-in timer breaks time into simple, visible sessions, gently reminding users when to start, pause, and reflect so they stay engaged with what they are doing.

Intended users. The primary users are students and others who need long periods of focused study, who want a simple, quick way to see how their time is passing and how their focus sessions are progressing.

Design & functionality. The BloomTimer is designed around a simple and calming interaction: users set a focus session, and the flower gradually opens as time progresses. A servo mechanism quietly drives each petal, while warm LED light increases to provide gentle feedback without causing distraction. The timer display keeps the user aware of their session, and the blooming motion acts as a small emotional reward for staying focused.

Process · Problem definition · Ideation · Sketches · Prototypes · Iterations

Design & Development Process

We documented each stage of our process, from defining the problem to building and refining multiple prototypes. Each step below shows one key phase and what we learned from it.

Step 1
Problem Definition

We needed a focus tool that avoids phone distractions and feels emotionally rewarding. This step clarified why existing phone-based timers were not enough and why a physical, ambient object could support focus better.

Step 2
Ideation

We brainstormed nine ideas around motivation and reward, and eventually chose the blooming flower metaphor. This helped us frame focus as growth over time instead of just a countdown.

Step 3
Sketches

We explored different petal shapes, opening paths and linkage concepts through quick hand sketches. These sketches helped us compare motion ideas before committing to any specific mechanism.

Step 4
Prototypes

Paper and cardboard models tested early motion: pushing, pulling, folding and layered openings. These low-fidelity prototypes revealed where friction and jamming would be a problem.

Step 5
Iterations

We compared vertical linkages versus rotating disks, refined friction points and printed multiple mechanism tests until the flower opened smoothly. Each iteration brought us closer to a motion that felt calm, reliable and bloom-like.

Internal structure · Mechanism · Electronics

Internal Structure

Inside the BloomTimer, a simple mechanical linkage and a single servo drive the opening motion, while an LED module and timer display provide feedback. The housing keeps the mechanism hidden so the user mainly sees the blooming flower and the soft glow of light.

The mechanism converts the servo’s rotation into vertical movement that lifts each petal. Electronics are built around a microcontroller, a timer display and warm white LEDs. Power and wiring are routed through the center of the flower to keep the exterior clean and maintain the illusion of a living object rather than a technical device.

How it works · User flow

How to Use BloomTimer

The interaction is intentionally simple so that the timer supports focus instead of competing for attention. A single focus session is represented by the gradual blooming of the flower.

1 · Setting Focus Time. Turn the control to set the desired focus duration on the timer display.
2 · Opening Flower. As the session progresses, the petals gradually open, showing how far you are into the focus block.
3 · Glowing Light. At the end of the session, the flower is fully open and the light glows as a quiet reward for staying focused.
